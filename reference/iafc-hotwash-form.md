# IAFC "Hotwash Form" — Lessons Learned from an Incident or Large-Scale Training Exercise

> Source: International Association of Fire Chiefs (IAFC), Terrorism and Homeland Security Committee — "Hotwash Form."
> Original PDF: `iafc_ths_committeeincidentsubmittalform.pdf` (in this directory).
> Submission: completed forms go to iafcthscommittee@iafc.org; when submitted by the fire chief of the jurisdiction, the committee posts the information on the IAFC THS Committee page for other chiefs to review and act on.

This is the fast, low-overhead capture instrument that exists **precisely because the formal After Action Report is too slow** — the same gap a closed-loop system targets.

---

## Intent (verbatim summary)

It is the intent of the committee to gather pertinent information related to significant incidents or large-scale training exercises **as soon as a fire chief (or designee) can do so**. The form's own justification:

> "Although After-Action-Reports are great sources of information on the entire incident, they can take **years** to get written and disseminated, **if they get written at all**."

What the Hotwash form provides is the means to collect:
- pertinent **lessons learned**,
- **challenges** that were faced, and
- **successes** that a fire chief and their community faced during a significant event or large-scale training exercise.

The information can be basic and to the point, and must always be respectful of sensitive issues the incident may have caused. The committee notes these forms create extra workload, but the captured information is "invaluable to helping all fire chiefs, their department members, and their communities... prepare for and respond to similar events."

## The form fields

### Type of incident
*(example: transportation accident, tornado, mass shooting, terror strike, etc.)*
- ☐ **Natural** — Describe: ___
- ☐ **Human-Caused** — Describe: ___

### Impact to
- **Community:** ___
- **Region:** ___
- **Emergency response agencies:** ___
- **Other(s):** ___

### Response
- **Initial response:** ___
- **1st operational period (1st 12 hours):** ___
- **Additional operational period(s):** ___

### Recovery
- **Recovery efforts:** ___

### Learning
- **Challenges faced at any point in the incident or recovery:** ___
- **Recommendations / suggestions / considerations to share:** ___

## Why this matters for a closed-loop system

The Hotwash form is a manual, paper-speed answer to the exact failure mode in the research: formal reports are slow or never written, so lessons evaporate. Its structure maps cleanly onto an automatable capture schema:

- **Context** (incident type, natural/human-caused) → tagging/routing
- **Impact** (community, region, agencies) → scope
- **Timeline** (initial / 12h / additional periods / recovery) → chronological reconstruction, matching FM 7-0 K-6 "what happened"
- **Challenges** → deficiencies to correct (FM 7-0 K-38)
- **Recommendations to share** → lessons to integrate and disseminate (FM 7-0 K-42–44)

The missing piece in both the IAFC form and the Army report is **tracking each recommendation to closure** (did it actually change a SOP/SOG/playbook, and was the next crew retrained?). That tracked-to-closure step is the NOEMA wedge.
