# Comparison — AAR / Training Closed-Loop Sources

> Cross-analysis of the four reference sources in this directory. See `README.md` for the index.
> Korean translation: `ko/comparison.md`.

Sources compared:
- **FM 7-0 App. K** — U.S. Army AAR doctrine (`fm-7-0-appendix-k-after-action-reviews.md`)
- **IAFC Hotwash** — fire-service fast-capture form (`iafc-hotwash-form.md`)
- **8-Step Model** — U.S. Army training-management cycle (`8-step-training-model.md`)
- **Fire Eng. AAR** — fire-service practitioner guide (`fire-service-aar-art.md`)

---

## 1. Core comparison matrix

| Dimension | **FM 7-0 App. K** (AAR doctrine) | **IAFC Hotwash form** | **8-Step Training Model** | **Fire Eng. "Art of the AAR"** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Domain | Military (US Army) | Fire service | Military (US Army) | Fire service |
| Document type | **Normative / doctrine** (must) | **Capture instrument / form** | **Process framework** | **Practitioner guide** |
| Primary purpose | Standardize *how* to run an AAR | Capture & disseminate lessons *fast* | *Cycle* a training event | Run an AAR *well* |
| Timing / speed | During ~ immediately after | Right after incident (within days) | Step 7 of the event | During ~ immediately after |
| Enforcement | High (doctrinal obligation) | Low (voluntary submission) | High (standard model) | Recommended (culture/norms) |
| Scope / subject | Individual, small unit → echelon | Organization, community, region | Company level and below | Company, shift, department |
| Formality | Both formal & informal | Single lightweight form | (absorbed by the AAR step) | Flexible, dynamic template |

## 2. Common ground (where all four converge)

| Shared element | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **The "four questions" learning structure** (what was supposed to / what actually / why / how next time) | FM 7-0 K-6 ≡ Fire Eng. four questions (1:1); implicit in Hotwash & 8-Step |
| **Avoid blaming individuals → focus on the situation** | FM 7-0 K-38 ("the platoon leader," not "2LT Wilson") ≡ Fire Eng. "fault vs responsibility" |
| **Lessons must be recorded & shared** | FM 7-0 K-42 & K-44, Hotwash "recommendations to share," Fire Eng. "lessons distributed" |
| **Roots in military Special Operations culture** | Fire Eng. states it explicitly; the AAR itself is military in origin |
| **Bias toward immediacy** (before the event goes cold) | AAR "during/immediately after," Hotwash "as soon as... possible" |
| **Common gap: no track-to-closure** | All four mandate capture/share only; actual doctrine/playbook revision depends on a human |

---

## 3. Differences (where they complement each other)

| Axis | One end | Other end |
|---|---|---|
| **Speed vs completeness** | Hotwash: fast but shallow (days) | After action report: complete but "takes years, if written at all" |
| **Enforcement vs voluntariness** | FM 7-0 & 8-Step: doctrinal mandate | Hotwash: chief's discretion |
| **Operating standard vs human factors** | FM 7-0: focus on *procedure/roles* | Fire Eng.: focus on *psychological safety / fear barriers* |
| **Single event vs cycle** | FM 7-0 & Fire Eng.: run one AAR | 8-Step: embeds AAR in a cycle, loops to *retrain* |
| **Individual learning vs org dissemination** | FM 7-0 (small-unit self-discovery) | Hotwash (posted/shared to other chiefs) |
| **Depth on psychological safety** | Fire Eng.: deepest (flatten rank, generational fears) | Hotwash & 8-Step: barely addressed |

---

## 4. Closed-loop coverage (the most important view)

How far each source takes responsibility along **capture → track → reflect**:

| Closed-loop stage | FM 7-0 K | Hotwash | 8-Step | Fire Eng. |
|---|:--:|:--:|:--:|:--:|
| ① Honest **capture** | ◐ (procedure) | ● (form) | ○ | ● (psych safety) |
| ② **Record & share** lessons | ● | ● | ◐ | ● |
| ③ **Track to closure** | ○ | ○ | ○ | ○ |
| ④ **Reflect** into doctrine/playbook | ◐ (only assigns K-13 senior trainer) | ○ | ◐ (only to retrain) | ○ |

`●` explicit responsibility · `◐` partial / role only assigned · `○` gap

**Bottom line:** the four sources cover ①②(capture/record) well, but **none owns ③ track-to-closure, and ④ org-wide reflection stops at "assign a person."** Those empty cells (③·④) are exactly where the NOEMA closed-loop wedge sits.
