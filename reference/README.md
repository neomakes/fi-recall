## The throughline

The training loop is **designed-closed** in doctrine:

> Plan → Execute/Train → **AAR (capture lessons)** → Evaluate → **Integrate & share** → Retrain → next planning cycle

But it **breaks in practice** between *lessons captured* and *doctrine/playbook actually updated*:
- Formal after action reports "take years to get written, if they get written at all" (IAFC).
- Action items rely on individual leader follow-through and aren't tracked to closure (Fire Engineering).
- Low psychological safety suppresses honest capture at the source (Fire Engineering).
- Doctrine assigns a senior trainer to integrate results (FM 7-0 K-13) and says lessons are "always recorded" (K-42), but organization-wide playbook revision is not mechanically forced.

**NOEMA wedge:** an automated closed-loop layer that lowers the cost of honest capture, then **tracks each lesson/action item to closure and reflects it back into the standing doctrine/playbook** — sitting exactly on FM 7-0 K-13 and K-42–44.
